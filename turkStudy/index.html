Graph layout



1. Browser

All modern ones, such as Safari, google chrome (best), and IE are supported.

2. Tasks

Biologists like to use the graph to trace information (the path or edges between nodes). Thus the only requirement in graph layout is to have a clear information flow, which can be achieved by re-arranging nodes and edges in the graph.

A couple of numbers are shown on the screen, such as edge crossing and downward edges to give you some sense of complexity. Please try to minimize these numbers, though an optimal solution is not always possible.

Please note that the "belonging" relationship cannot be changed. Each node lives in a box and it cannot be moved out of the box.

3. Video

Please watch this short video for instrutions on interaction. A couple of important ones include (1) moving a group by holding down 'G' to select and drag a group; (2) dragging the small rectangle (representing reaction in biology) by hold down the 'shift' key to move everything connected to it.
