2. Your Task

Your task is to re-arrange components in biological pathways to produce legible and aesthetically pleasing layout.

For example, Fig.2(a) is the original pathway graph whose name is "chromosome maintenance", and Fig.2(b) is the re-arranged one that obeys the rules in Section 3.1.

Fig.2 An Example Task and Result.

To make your layout acceptable, your graph MUST

  • contain less numbers of edge intersection than the time your start.
  • have no nodes overlapping.
  • have no nodes moving out of a compartment.

To reach these goals, please follow the instruction.

3. Introduction

A pathway graph is represented using compartment (blocks), nodes, and edges. Here a compartment(block) contains nodes and edges as well.
An example graph is shown below (Table.1):

A simple pathway graph Some illustrations

Compartments (blocks): G, H
Nodes: A, B, C , D, E, F
Edges: E->F, F->C, C->A, C->D, B->D
All edges are directional, which have a start point and an endpoint.

3.1 Rules

Here are some rules of thumb with examples that will help you re-arrange the graph to produce more legible and aesthetically pleasing layout.

Rules Operation Example Input Example Output
Avoid overlapping nodes Drag the overlapping node so nodes won't overlap.
Reduce edge intersection. Drag a node so edges won't intersect.
Reduce edge length. There are some unnecessarily long edges in the graphs, please drag the node (or a group of nodes) to shorten the edges.
Move a compartment If a compartment has no connections to any other compartments, move it to the top of the page.
Direction of the arrows Move nodes and compartment to make sure most arrows are pointing downwards.
Never ever change the containing relationships In your final graph, never place a node outside of a compartment it belongs to.

Table.2 Six rules.

To make a graph aesthetically pleasing, you may

  • make the nodes evenly distributed in the space.
  • avoid edge intersection and node overlapping by all means.
  • resize the compartment to take less screen space.
  • reize the compartment to fit better.
  • make sure the graph balance legibility and node distance or edge length.

3.2 Procedures

  • Please open the website through the link we give you in each task, After you open the website you will see an interface like this (see Fig.4 Interface below)
       (1) In the right corner of the screen is the menu bar, which has 4 types of buttons:
          Stop Force Direct: you can press this to stop automatic layout.
          Number of edge intersection: you can press this to detect how many edge intersection in the current graph.
          Your Worker ID: you should input your amazon mechnical turk worker Id to get the pay.
          Send: After you finish your work and input you worker ID, you can send the data to our server.
       (2) In the center of the interface, you will see many nodes in the compartment which are moving automatically. (We automatically layout the graph, when loading the graph)
  • After you find all the nodes become stable, or the number of the nodes overlapping and edges intersection is less, you can press the Stop Force Direct in the menubar to stop the automatic adjust. And then you can press any spare space in the screen, it will show you the graph you need to adjust.
  • You may begin by dragging the nodes and compartments by clicking left mouse. Compartment are also resizable. To do this, you can click the empty space in an compartment, you will see the compartment turns yellow and eight red-colored widgets on the edges. You may drag the widgets to resize it). You can also select a group of nodes by holding down the "Ctrl" key on your keyboard while using left-mouse selection.
  • You may save intermedidate results. To save your result, put your "worker ID" in the menu bar which is located in the upper right corner of the screen and then click the "send" button.
  • You can click "Number of edge intersection" to test whether or not your layout is valid (fewer the better.) The original number of edge intersection is displayed on the screen already.
  • When you are done, again, put your "worker ID" and click the "send" button.
  • Just in case you put in a wrong "worker ID", simply put it again.
